Isaac Legene

I’m a Master’s student in Robotics with a focus on control systems, reinforcement learning, and humanoid robotics. My work sits at the intersection of simulation, learning-based control, and real-world deployment.

I have hands-on experience building and training locomotion and manipulation policies in simulation, particularly using NVIDIA Isaac Lab and modern RL frameworks. I’ve worked extensively with velocity tracking, stand-up behaviors, curriculum learning, and manager-based RL environments for legged and humanoid robots.

Beyond research, I have industry experience in manufacturing and automation, including virtual commissioning, industrial simulation, and control systems. I’m comfortable working across the stack — from low-level C++ and Python robotics code to higher-level system design and experimentation.

My interests include humanoid locomotion, robot learning, simulation-to-real transfer, and building robust, field-ready robotic systems.
